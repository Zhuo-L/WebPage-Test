Intelligence beyond a single LLM

Scientific intelligence demands fine-grained, long-horizon reasoning that is difficult to compress into a single model. Inspired by the Extended Mind Thesis, ExoMind organizes intelligence as an agentic system of an LLM, typed interaction objects, and autonomous interaction processes.

A different way to scale scientific intelligence

01

Shift specialization from repeatedly compressing heterogeneous tasks into model parameters toward designing interaction objects around scientific processes.

02

New scaling dimensions

Expand capability through richer interaction objects and deeper autonomous interaction, beyond model size, data volume, and training time.

03

Scientific process capacity

Represent evidence acquisition, executable verification, and long-horizon scientific workflows as part of the intelligence system itself.

Building ExoMind

ExoMind is built through three connected systems rather than a single training recipe: data engineering determines what is worth learning, interaction design determines how scientific work is performed, and progressive training aligns the model with that process.

Systematic data engineering

A multidisciplinary pool of approximately 60K scientific problems is filtered by quality, predicted difficulty, and tool benefit before trajectory generation. Medium-to-hard problems are then routed to pure-reasoning or interaction-reasoning paths according to their training value.

Deeply specialized scientific interaction

Typed interaction objects turn atomic scientific abilities into executable actions. Chain-of-Interaction distillation and outcome, rule, and process verification produce high-quality long-horizon trajectories and reusable scientific interaction patterns.

Systematic training strategy

A shared interface preserves training-inference consistency. Two-stage hybrid progressive CoI training first builds intrinsic reasoning and basic interaction ability, then internalizes higher-quality interaction patterns under a low-cost setting.

Emergent scientific interaction patterns

More interaction objects do not automatically produce better scientific reasoning. ExoMind learns to organize finite atomic capabilities into observation-guided processes: it identifies the current uncertainty, selects an appropriate interaction object, absorbs the returned evidence or executable feedback, and updates subsequent reasoning. These reusable processes emerge as verification-seeking, evidence-seeking, and hybrid closed-loop patterns.

  1. 01
    Locate uncertainty

    Identify a source, knowledge, or algebraic gap.

  2. 02
    Select interaction

    Retrieve, browse, or execute according to that gap.

  3. 03
    Absorb observation

    Ground evidence or verify a fragile derivation.

  4. 04
    Update reasoning

    Feed the observation into the next decision and synthesis.

FrontierScience-Olympiad #04

Resolve a sign ambiguity through executable verification

1.0 / 1.0

Derive the electric field outside a grounded conducting cylinder in a uniform transverse field and verify that the result satisfies the physical boundaries.

  1. FrameExpress the potential in cylindrical coordinates.
  2. DeriveDifferentiate radial and angular components.
  3. InspectIdentify uncertainty in the tangential sign.
  4. VerifyRun symbolic differentiation in the code executor.
  5. ResolveCorrect the sign and check boundary and far-field limits.
Trajectory details

Action

Er = -diff(Phi, r)
Etheta = -(1/r) * diff(Phi, theta)

Observation

Er = E0*(a**2 + r**2)*cos(theta)/r**2
Etheta = E0*(a**2 - r**2)*sin(theta)/r**2

The symbolic result exposes the sign error directly; ExoMind updates the derivation before checking the conductor boundary and the asymptotic field.

FrontierScience-Research #19

Close the loop between literature and mathematical verification

10.0 / 10.0

Construct a quantum shutter protocol and generalize the one-photon mechanism to an N-photon setting while preserving the required orthogonality.

  1. RetrieveFind the quantum shutter construction in the literature.
  2. ExtractGround the state equations and constraints.
  3. GeneralizeExtend the construction to K shutters and K photons.
  4. ComputeVerify the inner-product condition programmatically.
  5. SynthesizePresent a source-grounded and algebraically checked result.
Trajectory details

Evidence

The trajectory retrieves quant-ph/0206074 and extracts the relevant construction and orthogonality equations.

Verification

inner = (m - D) / (2*N - m)
substitute D = m  ->  inner = 0

External evidence supplies the construction; executable algebra tests the generalization before the final answer joins both strands into one argument.
